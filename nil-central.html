Rules & Regulations

Each state operates under a different governing body. Understanding your state's rules protects your eligibility.

Protect Your Eligibility

Quick Tips

The most important things every high school athlete needs to know before entering any NIL deal.

📋

Always Disclose First

Disclose all NIL deals to your school's athletic director before signing anything. Failure to disclose can jeopardize your eligibility.

🚫

No School Marks

Never use your school's logo, uniform, colors, or mascot in an NIL deal without explicit written permission from your school principal and AD.

💰

It's Taxable Income

All NIL compensation is taxable. Once you start earning, consult a tax professional. Your parents or guardians should be involved in this process.

📝

Get It In Writing

Verbal agreements are not enforceable. Every NIL deal needs a written contract. If you're a minor, a parent or guardian must co-sign.

⚠️

No Pay-for-Play

NIL compensation must be for your name, image, and likeness only — never tied to your athletic performance, stats, wins, or school enrollment decisions.

🏷️

Label Sponsored Content

Any paid social media post must clearly be labeled as a paid partnership using #ad or #sponsored. This is required by the FTC regardless of your age.

📱

Social Media = Your Brand

Brands look at your social media before reaching out. Keep your public accounts professional, clean, and football-focused. Your digital footprint matters.

⭐

Know Your Value First

Use the NIL Rating Tool above to understand your market value before approaching any brand or signing any deal. Don't negotiate blind.
